# Context — Domain Glossary

Shared vocabulary for this repo of 3D-printable automotive accessories. One term
per entry, definitions only — no implementation details or specs.

## Caterham Seven
Lightweight British roadster. The user runs a **420R S3**; "S3" is the
narrow/standard chassis (as opposed to the wider "SV").

## Windscreen
The full-width, gently curved laminated-glass front screen in an aluminium
frame, carried on two **stanchions**. Removable for track use. (British
"windscreen" = US "windshield" — used interchangeably here.)

## Aeroscreen
A small GRP wind deflector fitted in place of the **windscreen** for track days.
Not the object this mount holds.

## Stanchion
One of the two flat steel plates that carry the **windscreen**: a long arm
(bolted to the frame along a row of holes) widening to a triangular foot that
normally bolts to the **scuttle**. The foot has a **triangular cutout** — a
triangular through-hole used, in this project, as the hanging feature: the wall
mount's **arm** passes through it so the screen hangs by its two stanchions,
keeping all load off the glass and frame.

## Arm (mount)
The load-bearing limb of a wall bracket. Because the **stanchion**'s triangular
cutout faces *perpendicular* to the wall, the arm projects out from the wall and
then turns 90° into a sideways **peg** that passes through the cutout. The peg is
a cylinder with a slightly larger-diameter **collar** at its outer end (a *lip*):
the inverted screen's cutout drops over the peg and hangs on it, and the collar
keeps the stanchion from sliding off the end. The screen hangs from two arms
(one per stanchion, on two identical brackets).

## Scuttle
The body panel behind the bonnet that the **stanchion** feet bolt to on the car.
Referenced only to describe the stanchion — not part of the mount.
