So the correct layout is: tunnel → firewall (vertical face at the front) → dash sits higher with an overhang above. The tray sits on the tunnel and its forward-most wall presses against the firewall, using the same velcro attachment as the fusebox cover.

This is a great solution

Velcro on the tray's front face against the firewall is smart because:

  • The firewall is always there — it's a fixed reference surface. The tray self-locates just by sliding forward until it touches.
  • Braking loads push into the firewall — velcro in compression + shear is very strong.
  • Lateral loads — a couple of Dual Lock patches on the tunnel floor under the tray stop side-to-side sliding.
  • No drilling, totally reversible — same as the factory fusebox cover approach.
  • The velcro takes almost zero peel during normal use — the tray sits on the tunnel, gravity holds it down, and the firewall stops it sliding forward.

For the tunnel floor, I'd use Dual Lock (not standard velcro) — it's more rigid in shear and doesn't let the tray "rock" on the fuzzy loop pile. The front wall can use standard velcro (hook side on the tray, loop on the firewall, or vice versa) to match whatever the fusebox cover uses.
