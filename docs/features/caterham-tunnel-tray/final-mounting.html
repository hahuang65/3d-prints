Friction patches + front velcro — that works

A quick load check so we know it's not going to slide around on track:

Lateral (cornering) ✓

Silicone/rubber on smooth leather has a friction coefficient of ~0.6–0.8. A loaded tray (~500 g) resists ~3–4 N of lateral force from friction alone. Plus the front velcro patch also adds lateral shear resistance. 1 G cornering = ~5 N. Close but adequate.

Braking (forward) ✓✓

The tray pushes into the firewall. The velcro is in pure compression. This is the strongest load case — the tray physically can't move forward.

Acceleration (rearward) ✓

Friction resists rearward sliding. The velcro doesn't help here (it's in peel when pulling away from the firewall). But under acceleration, loads are lower than braking (~0.5 G vs ~1 G).

Lift / knee bump ⚠️

The front velcro sees peel if the rear lifts. Mitigation: low tapered rear edge on both tray and lid so knees slide over, not under. A 15–20 mm rear wall with a 45° chamfer is the key detail here.

Margin of safety: If lateral grip isn't enough on track, you could add a single narrow velcro strip running fore-aft down the centre of the tray floor, with the loop side stuck to the leather (it's just a fuzzy strip, unobtrusive, removable). But try the pure friction approach first — I think it'll be fine for the weight of a phone, wallet and keys.

Friction material options

All of these stick to the underside of the tray — none touch the leather directly with adhesive:

Material Source Friction on leather Thickness
Clear silicone bumpers Cabinet-drawer pads, Amazon, hardware store High 2–3 mm
3M Bumpon™ (SJ5741) Self-adhesive rubber bumpers Very high 1.5 mm
Silicone sheet / mat Shelf liner, cut to shape High 1–2 mm
EVA foam Craft store, thin tool drawer liner Medium 2–3 mm

My pick: 3M Bumpon — they're thin, incredibly grippy, have aggressive adhesive on one side, and come in small dots/strips so you can place them strategically without covering the whole floor. Four 15×15 mm squares at the corners of the tray floor would be plenty.

Summary of the final design

  • Mounting: Velcro on tray front wall → firewall. Silicone/rubber friction pads on tray underside → leather tunnel. No adhesive on the leather.
